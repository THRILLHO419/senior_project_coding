New York City

For my final piece for the Local Music and Local Politics series, I discussed the history of New York City’s infamous “cabaret law.” The law banned dancing in venues that did not possess a “cabaret license,” which was infamously hard to get and maintain. The law was passed during the Prohibition era to crack down on speakeasies, but stayed on the books for 91 years, until it was repealed in 2017. This originally aired on March 9, 2018.
