Austin

For my first piece in the Local Music and Local Politics series, I decided to start by reaching out to some Austin musicians. I was fortunate enough to know three people who work in Austin’s music industry as musicians, singers, and producers. In addition, they work as high school educators. I figured that it would be interesting to hear their perspective on what works and what doesn’t in Austin’s music scene, so I sat down with them individually, recorded interviews, and assembled the insights I found to be most striking into this piece. This originally aired on October 6, 2017.



For my second piece in the Local Music and Local Politics series, I continued with Austin to comment on data from the Austin Music Census, a large survey conducted by the City of Austin. This survey asked those who work in Austin’s music economy in many professions, both full and part-time, some qualitative and quantitative questions. In my piece, I summarized the survey and gave description and commentary on the data points that I regarded as particularly interesting, surprising, or notable. This originally aired on November 3, 2017.
